# Q1 2024 Churn Analysis
**Period:** January - March 2024

## Summary
Overall churn rate for Q1 2024 was **4.2%**, up from 3.8% in Q4 2023. The increase was driven primarily by the SMB segment, which saw churn climb to **6.1%**. Enterprise churn remained stable at **1.4%**.

## Key Findings

### 1. SMB Segment
SMB customers who did not complete onboarding within the first 14 days churned at a rate of **9.3%**, compared to **2.8%** for those who completed onboarding. This suggests onboarding completion is a strong leading indicator of retention in this segment.

### 2. Feature Adoption
Customers using 3 or more core features had a churn rate of **1.9%**. Customers using only 1 feature churned at **7.4%**. The reporting and dashboard features showed the strongest correlation with retention.

### 3. Support Tickets
Customers who submitted more than 2 unresolved support tickets in a 30-day window churned at **3x the baseline rate**. Response time above 48 hours was associated with a 40% increase in churn probability.

### 4. Pricing
28% of churned customers cited pricing as a factor in exit surveys. However, customers on annual contracts churned at **1.1%**, compared to **5.6%** for month-to-month customers.

## Recommendations
- Invest in SMB onboarding automation to reduce time-to-completion
- Implement proactive outreach for customers with low feature adoption after 30 days
- Reduce support ticket resolution time as a priority retention lever
- Incentivize annual contract conversion for month-to-month customers

## Methodology
Analysis based on 12,400 active accounts. Churn defined as cancellation or non-renewal within the period. Cohort analysis used to control for account age. Exit survey response rate was 34%.
